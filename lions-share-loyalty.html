How the Program Works

MoneyLion's Lion's Share is a monthly rebate program where members earn cashback based on their engagement. The more you use the app, the higher your tier and the bigger your reward.

Members earn their rebate by:

Making purchases with RoarMoney
Logging into the app daily
Bronze $2
Silver $5
Gold $10
Platinum $19.99

The Problem

Despite being a genuine benefit, the program had no dedicated experience. Cashback was buried in transaction history and members had no way to track their progress or understand what tier they were on.

An incentive program nobody could see

Without visibility into progress or rewards, members had no reason to change their behaviour. The program existed, but it wasn't driving the engagement it was designed for.

Outcome

+7.6% Increase in average RoarMoney purchases
+0.7% Increase in average login days per month

Decision 1: From Buried to Visible

From a buried line item to a live progress widget

The original experience hid cashback inside transaction history with no way to see tier status or remaining progress. The opportunity was clear: surface it where members already look, and make it feel alive.

Before vs After — Progress widget

Before — buried in rewards history
After — dedicated progress widget
Improvement

The widget gives members a persistent, at-a-glance view of their tier progress — transforming a passive benefit into an active motivation loop.

1. Progress at a glance

Members can now see exactly where they stand in their current tier cycle without navigating away from their main dashboard. The visual bar makes progress feel tangible and close.

2. A moment of delight

Reaching a new tier triggers an animation, turning a routine financial milestone into a rewarding moment. Small celebrations reinforce the behaviour we want to encourage.

3. Refresh date keeps urgency real

Showing the cycle reset date creates gentle time pressure, giving members a reason to act before progress resets rather than treating the program as a passive background benefit.

Decision 2: Edge Cases

The widget had to communicate more than progress

Not every member is actively progressing. Some are close to achieving their tier, others haven't engaged at all. The widget needed to handle each state with clarity, not just the happy path.

Edge cases — Progress widget

Active
Achieving
Inactive
Improvement

Each state uses distinct visual language so members immediately understand their situation and what action, if any, is available to them.

1. Active state sets the baseline expectation

Members who are on track see their progress bar filling up with a clear indication of where they sit within the tier. The focus is on momentum, not instruction.

2. Achieving state nudges at the right moment

When a member is close to the next tier, the widget surfaces a contextual prompt to close the gap. Timing this message when it's still achievable makes it motivating rather than frustrating.

3. Inactive state explains without shaming

Members who haven't earned anything this cycle see a neutral, informative state rather than a visually empty or broken-looking widget. The design preserves trust while gently re-engaging them.

Implementation

Built with Rive State Machine

The progress animation and tier transition moments were built using Rive, enabling smooth, interactive state-driven motion without heavy video assets.
State machines allowed the widget to respond to real membership data dynamically, switching between active, achieving, and inactive states at runtime.
The engineering handoff included annotated state diagrams to ensure the Rive logic translated accurately into the production app.
